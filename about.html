Our Story

Built by
people who
know fabric.

The Fabric Connector was founded by Bas and Bart Slootman — a father and son who between them bring over three decades of textile industry experience and a shared belief that sourcing doesn't have to be a compromise.

Bart spent thirty years watching the industry produce more than it needed. Bas grew up inside that world, then studied Business & Social Impact, and came back with a question: what if responsible sourcing was the default, not the exception?

We started in Amsterdam with a physical material library and a straightforward goal — to make it easier for design-driven brands to find the right fabric, from the right source, without the usual friction. Natural fibres, next-generation materials, and carefully curated deadstock: all in one place, with a team that understands what you're making.

We've since built a network of 200+ mill partners across 40+ countries, launched an impact measurement tool, and joined platforms like Munich Fabric Start's ReSOURCE. But the mission stays the same: fabric sourcing for brands that care about both quality and consequence.

How We
Think About
Sourcing.

We don't believe responsible sourcing has to come at the cost of design. The brands we work with prove otherwise every season — great materials, clear provenance, and collections that look and feel the way they should.

Our approach is consultative, not transactional. We take the brief, understand the collection, and find fabrics that serve both the creative and the conscience. That means being honest when something isn't right — and relentless when it comes to finding something that is.

01 — Natural First

Start With Nature

Linen, wool, organic cotton, hemp, silk — grown and processed with traceability and care. Our default is natural, because the best materials often already exist in the ground.

02 — Look Forward

Embrace What's Next

Mycelium, algae fibres, bio-based alternatives — we scout the frontier of material innovation so your brand can lead. Not every brand is ready for next-gen. But we make sure you know when you are.

03 — Nothing Wasted

Deadstock as a Choice

The most sustainable fabric is still the one that already exists. Deadstock isn't a fallback — it's a creative decision. Limited runs, premium mills, materials that tell a story.
